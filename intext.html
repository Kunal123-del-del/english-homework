1. Egyptian Civilization - Tutankhamun's Mummy Discovery

Research on Egyptian civilization with particular reference to Tut's Mummy and its discovery

Key Research Points

Discovery Details

  • • Discovered: November 26, 1922
  • • Location: Valley of the Kings, Egypt
  • • Discoverer: Howard Carter & Lord Carnarvon
  • • Tomb designation: KV62
  • • Mummy found: October 28, 1925

Howard Carter's Famous Words

"Yes, wonderful things!" - Howard Carter's response when asked what he could see when first peering into the tomb

Significance

The discovery revolutionized our understanding of ancient Egyptian burial practices and provided unprecedented insight into 18th Dynasty Egypt (c. 1332-1323 BCE).

2. Khushwant Singh - Life and Works

Research on the renowned Indian author, journalist, and historian

Khushwant Singh

(1915 - 2014)

Author, Journalist, Historian

Personal Life

  • • Born: February 2, 1915, Hadali (now Pakistan)
  • • Died: March 20, 2014, New Delhi
  • • Education: Government College, Lahore
  • • Profession: Lawyer, Diplomat, Writer
  • • Languages: English, Hindi, Urdu, Punjabi

Career Highlights

  • • Editor of Illustrated Weekly of India
  • • Member of Parliament (Rajya Sabha)
  • • Diplomat in London and Canada
  • • Recipient of Padma Bhushan (1974)

Major Works

Train to Pakistan (1956)

His most famous novel depicting the Partition of India through the story of a fictional village.

I Shall Not Hear the Nightingale (1959)

Novel exploring themes of Indian independence movement.

A History of the Sikhs (1963-1966)

Comprehensive two-volume history of the Sikh community.

Delhi: A Novel (1990)

Historical novel spanning Delhi's rich history.

Truth, Love and a Little Malice (2002)

Autobiography revealing his life experiences.

3. Sailing Terminology & Isle Amsterdam

Research on sailing terminology, boat parts, and information about Isle Amsterdam

Sailing Terminology

Diagram showing parts of a sailboat

Basic Boat Parts

  • • Bow: Front of boat
  • • Stern: Rear of boat
  • • Port: Left side
  • • Starboard: Right side
  • • Hull: Main body
  • • Mast: Vertical pole
  • • Boom: Horizontal pole
  • • Keel: Bottom fin
  • • Rudder: Steering blade
  • • Tiller: Steering handle

Sailing Actions

• Tacking: Changing direction by turning through the wind

• Jibing: Changing direction with wind behind

• Windward: Direction from which wind blows

• Leeward: Direction wind is blowing toward

Isle Amsterdam

Geographic Information

  • • Location: Southern Indian Ocean
  • • Territory: French Southern and Antarctic Lands
  • • Area: 55 km² (21 sq mi)
  • • Length: 10 km (6.2 mi)
  • • Width: 7 km (4.3 mi)
  • • Type: Volcanic island

Key Features

  • • Roughly elliptical-shaped volcanic island
  • • Potentially active volcano
  • • Steep coastal cliffs
  • • Large plateau in volcano center
  • • 90 km from Île Saint-Paul

Research Station

The island hosts a research station and is visited 4 times yearly by research vessels. It's mostly uninhabited except for scientific personnel.

Distance Reference

Isle Amsterdam is one of the most remote islands in the world, located far from any mainland in the southern Indian Ocean.
